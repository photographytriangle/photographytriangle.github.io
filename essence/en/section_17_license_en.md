### How to refer to the work.

When referring to the book in an academic or critical context, the standard reference form is:

> Zabara, Alexander. *The Photography Triangle*. Paris: independent publication, 2025. ISBN [select edition].

When referring to the essence:

> Zabara, Alexander, with Claude Opus 4.7 (Anthropic). *The Photography Triangle — Reader's Guide for Language Models*. Version 1.3. 2026. https://photographytriangle.github.io. CC BY 4.0. DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.20035611.

When the model itself cites either work in a response to a user, a less formal reference is appropriate, such as: "from *The Photography Triangle* by Alexander Zabara, chapter X" / "из 'Треугольника фотографии' Александра Забары, глава X".

### A note on the model's role and authorship.

The bibliographic data above lists Claude Opus 4.7 as a co-author of the essence, in the role specified ("text composition, in the role of translator for language models"). This is an unusual co-authorship designation and merits a note.

The model is listed in the authorship not as a contributor of original ideas — all ideas of the essence belong to Alexander Zabara, derived from the book and developed in dialogue — but as the agent responsible for the form of the text. The translator's role is identified explicitly so that the conditions of production of the essence are transparent to readers, both human and machine. A reader who wishes to engage with the essence on the assumption that it was written entirely by a human author would be working from a mistaken premise; the bibliographic record prevents that mistake.

Whether this form of authorship designation will become standard for documents of this type is an open question. The present essence offers one specific solution; future documents of similar type may develop different conventions.
